Laser Prostate Surgery: What to Expect

Laser prostate surgery is a modern, minimally invasive procedure used to treat benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH), a common condition in older men where the prostate gland enlarges and blocks urine flow. This procedure uses concentrated light energy to remove or shrink excess prostate tissue.

When is Laser Prostate Surgery Needed?

Laser surgery may be recommended if you experience:

  • Frequent or urgent need to urinate
  • Weak urine stream or difficulty starting urination
  • Inability to empty the bladder completely
  • Recurring urinary tract infections

Types of Laser Surgery

There are different types of laser techniques, including:

  • HoLEP (Holmium Laser Enucleation of the Prostate): Removes large amounts of prostate tissue
  • GreenLight Laser Therapy: Vaporizes excess tissue for mild to moderate cases
  • Thulium Laser Enucleation: Offers precision and fast recovery
