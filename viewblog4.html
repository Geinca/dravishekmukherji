BPH (Enlarged Prostate) Treatment Options

Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia (BPH), or an enlarged prostate, is a common condition in aging men. While not cancerous, BPH can significantly impact quality of life by affecting urination and bladder function. Fortunately, there are multiple treatment options depending on severity and individual health needs.

Recognizing BPH Symptoms

  • Frequent urination, especially at night (nocturia)
  • Weak or interrupted urine stream
  • Urgency to urinate
  • Difficulty starting urination
  • Inability to completely empty the bladder

Diagnosis and Evaluation

BPH is diagnosed through a combination of medical history, digital rectal exam (DRE), urine flow studies, and ultrasound. PSA blood tests may also be conducted to rule out prostate cancer.

Treatment Options for BPH

  1. Lifestyle Changes: Limiting caffeine, alcohol, and evening fluid intake can ease symptoms.
  2. Medications: Alpha-blockers and 5-alpha-reductase inhibitors help relax the prostate and shrink its size.

With timely medical advice and tailored treatment, most men experience significant relief from BPH symptoms. Consult a urologist to determine the best option based on your specific needs and health condition.
