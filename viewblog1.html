Understanding Kidney Stones: Causes & Prevention

Kidney stones are hard deposits of minerals and salts that form inside your kidneys. They can cause severe pain, especially when passing through the urinary tract, and can lead to infections or kidney damage if untreated.

Common Causes of Kidney Stones

Kidney stones form when your urine contains more crystal-forming substances—like calcium, oxalate, and uric acid—than the fluid in your urine can dilute. Risk factors include:

  • Dehydration: Not drinking enough water daily
  • Diet: High intake of protein, salt, and sugar
  • Obesity: Increases risk of stone formation
  • Medical conditions: Such as hyperparathyroidism and urinary tract infections

Signs and Symptoms

While small stones may pass unnoticed, larger stones can cause:

  • Severe pain in the back or lower abdomen
  • Blood in urine
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Frequent urination or burning sensation

Prevention Tips

  1. Stay Hydrated: Drink at least 8–10 glasses of water daily
  2. Reduce Sodium: Limit salt intake
  3. Eat More Citrate-Rich Foods: Like lemons and oranges
  4. Limit Oxalate-Rich Foods: Such as spinach, beets, and nuts
  5. Consult a Specialist: For tailored dietary and medical advice

Understanding your risk factors and making small dietary and lifestyle changes can help prevent kidney stones. If you experience symptoms, consult a urologist promptly.
